Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Get answers to common questions about our financial calculators and investment planning tools.

What is SIP and how does the SIP calculator work?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing in mutual funds through regular monthly contributions. Our SIP calculator uses compound interest formula to project future returns based on your monthly investment, expected return rate, and investment duration.

How accurate are the investment calculator results?

Our calculators provide estimates based on mathematical formulas and your input parameters. Actual returns may vary due to market conditions, fund performance, and economic factors. These tools are for planning purposes and should not be considered as guaranteed returns.

What is the difference between SIP and lump sum investment?

SIP involves regular monthly investments that help average out market volatility, while lump sum is a one-time investment. SIP is ideal for salary earners, while lump sum works when you have surplus funds to invest immediately.

Can I use these calculators for tax planning?

While our calculators help estimate returns, they don't include tax implications. Consult a tax advisor for comprehensive tax planning including ELSS investments, tax deductions under Section 80C, and capital gains tax considerations.

What is a good return rate to expect from investments?

Historical equity mutual fund returns in India have ranged from 10-15% annually over long periods. However, returns vary by asset class: equity funds (10-15%), debt funds (6-9%), and hybrid funds (8-12%). Choose rates based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

How often should I review my investment calculations?

Review your financial goals and recalculate annually or when your income changes significantly. Market conditions and personal circumstances change, so regular reviews help ensure you're on track to meet your financial objectives.
